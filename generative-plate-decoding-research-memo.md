# Generative Trellis Decoding for License Plate Recognition Under Severe Degradation

**Working title:** *An Ideal-Observer Approach to Low-Resolution License Plate Recognition: Physics-Based Forward Models, Trellis-Factorized Decoding, and Fundamental Performance Bounds*

**Status:** v2 — revised after first deep-research review (July 2026). Broad novelty claims N2/N3 retired; lineage acknowledged (Kopec–Chou DID, Bulan HMM-ALPR, Hsieh low-res template matching, EM barcode decoding); trellis/bound contradiction fixed (§4.2, §4.7). Primary thesis now: **construct the first task-specific ideal observer for severe LRLPR, quantify the statistical efficiency of modern recognizers against it, and show when information is present even though reconstruction is impossible.**

**Date:** July 2026

---

## 1. One-Paragraph Summary

License plate recognition under severe degradation (low resolution, blur, compression) is currently dominated by discriminative learning: train networks on degraded images to output character strings. This memo proposes the inverse direction: since the hypothesis space is small, discrete, and fully enumerable (a plate is one of ~10^8 strings rendered in a known, government-mandated font on known geometry), decode by **analysis-by-synthesis** — render candidate plates through a high-fidelity physical forward model (optics → sensor → ISP → codec), and score hypotheses by likelihood against the observed pixels. Blur locality factorizes the likelihood into a chain graphical model, making exact MAP decoding tractable (Viterbi over ~36 states per character position). Multi-frame video fusion becomes exact log-likelihood summation across frames rather than heuristic voting. The same machinery yields (a) calibrated confidence as a likelihood ratio, (b) a self-auditing consistency test for forward-model validity, and (c) — most importantly — the **ideal-observer performance bound** for the task: the Bayes-optimal recognition rate as a function of degradation parameters, against which all existing methods can be measured for statistical efficiency. To our knowledge, no published work assembles this complete system or publishes this bound. The primary request of reviewers: **verify or refute the novelty claims in §7.**

## 2. Problem Setting

Low-Resolution License Plate Recognition (LRLPR) is an active, unsolved problem with forensic relevance. Reference points:

- The ICPR 2026 Competition on LRLPR (arXiv:2604.22506; Codabench competition 12259) used the LRLPR-26 dataset: tracks of 5 consecutive low-res crops per plate (Brazilian + Mercosur layouts), 20k training tracks with paired HR images, 3k test tracks. 269 registered teams, 99 valid final submissions. Winner: 82.13% exact-match recognition rate; participant mean 61.3%. Secondary metric: Confidence Gap (mean confidence on correct minus incorrect predictions), which varied ~10× among teams with near-identical accuracy.
- The competition organizers' summary finding: no single architecture dominated; the consistent ingredient among top teams was effective use of the 5-frame track structure (temporal fusion, voting, logit aggregation, ensembling).
- Prior literature reports SOTA at 50–60% on real-world low-quality single images.
- All top-5 competition approaches were discriminative (learned recognizers, optionally with learned super-resolution front-ends). None used explicit generative/likelihood-based decoding.

## 3. Core Claim

For a hypothesis space that is (i) discrete, (ii) small enough to enumerate or factorize, and (iii) generated by a known deterministic renderer (font, layout, geometry mandated by law), the Bayes decoder over rendered hypotheses is the **ideal observer**: no estimator, neural or otherwise, achieves lower error probability *given the correct forward model*. The direction of inference is knowledge → degradation (render candidates, degrade them, compare to observation), not degradation → knowledge (enhance the observation, then read it). This recovers information in regimes where reconstruction is impossible: legibility is not required for classification, because distinguishing ~10^8 structured hypotheses requires far less SNR than rendering legible glyphs (a Mercosur plate carries ~27 bits; reconstruction-quality SNR is orders of magnitude more demanding).

The proof-of-concept for this direction already exists in an adjacent field: text redaction attacks (Depix; DepixHMM implementing Hill, Zhou, Saul & Shacham, *"On the (In)effectiveness of Mosaicing and Blurring as Tools for Document Redaction"*; Bishop Fox's Unredacter) recover pixelated/blurred known-font text by rendering candidates and matching — and led the security community to conclude that any such obfuscation of known-font text is broken in principle. This memo's thesis is that the same principle, upgraded with a physically faithful camera forward model, applies to license plates under real-world optical/sensor/codec degradation, and that this has not been assembled and benchmarked against the modern discriminative SOTA.

## 4. Formal Framework

### 4.1 Notation

Let s = (c₁,…,c₇) be the plate string over alphabet A (|A| ≈ 36, layout-dependent). Let R(s; g) be a renderer producing an ideal image of plate s under geometric parameters g (homography from plate plane to image, including per-row rolling-shutter shear). Let θ denote nuisance parameters: PSF (defocus ⊗ motion), photometric field (local gain/offset), noise parameters (shot/read), demosaic model, codec quantization state. The observation model for frame y:

p(y | s, θ, g) = Noise( Codec( ISP( PSF ∗ R(s; g) ; θ ) ) )

### 4.2 Trellis factorization

Blur is spatially local: character cᵢ influences only a bounded image region, with coupling to neighbors only where PSF supports overlap at character boundaries. The log-likelihood therefore decomposes as unary terms per character position plus pairwise terms between adjacent positions:

log p(y | s, θ, g) ≈ Σᵢ φᵢ(cᵢ) + Σᵢ ψᵢ(cᵢ, cᵢ₊₁)

**This factorization is an approximation, not the definition of the decoder.** Wide PSFs couple a character to more than one neighbor; JPEG blocks, resampling, sharpening, and tone mapping can couple multiple character regions; video inter-prediction couples frames. The hierarchy is therefore: (a) the **exact ideal observer** is defined by the complete forward likelihood p(y|s) and computed by exhaustive enumeration — feasible, because compositional rendering (render each glyph in each position once, compose per hypothesis; only block-local ISP/codec nonlinearities require joint evaluation) makes scoring ~10^8 format-constrained hypotheses a GPU-hours computation, not an open problem; (b) an **augmented trellis** whose state stores the previous k characters (|A|^k states; k=2 → 1,296, still trivial for Viterbi) captures wider coupling exactly up to k-neighbor range; (c) the **pairwise trellis** is the fast approximate decoder: O(7 · |A|²) after computing unary/pairwise tables. The measured **factorization gap** between (a) and (b)/(c) on reduced alphabets and full plates is itself a methodological contribution. Layout/format priors (e.g., Mercosur LLL-D-L-DD; US state-specific formats) enter as hard constraints at every level. Full posteriors, marginals, and top-k lists come from forward-backward at the same cost.

### 4.3 Joint nuisance estimation (the discreteness advantage)

Blind deconvolution is classically ill-posed: joint estimation of an unconstrained image and PSF admits infinite trade-offs. Constraining the "image" to the discrete rendered set breaks this: alternate (a) decode s given current θ̂, ĝ (Viterbi pass); (b) refine θ, ĝ by fitting R(ŝ) through the forward model to y (continuous optimization, low-dimensional). This is EM with a Viterbi E-step. The plate is simultaneously the unknown and its own calibration target — possible only because the unknown is discrete and tiny.

Additionally, much of θ and g is identifiable from scene structure independent of s: plate border and character stems act as slanted-edge MTF targets (local PSF estimation); plate corners give the homography; track velocity gives rolling-shutter shear; JPEG headers or DCT-coefficient histograms give codec state; photon-transfer analysis on flat patches gives the noise model.

### 4.4 Nuisance taxonomy (design discipline)

Every candidate effect is binned by identifiability and leverage:

1. **Identifiable from the observation → estimate** (PSF, homography, rolling shutter, codec state, noise parameters, demosaic phase).
2. **Influential but unidentifiable → marginalize** (windshield/dust perturbations, intra-exposure trajectory, shimmer): p(y|s) = ∫ p(y|s,θ) p(θ) dθ over a low-dimensional (~10–20d) perturbation prior. Deterministic modeling of unidentifiable effects is worse than marginalizing them (confident systematic error vs. honest posterior widening).
3. **Sub-threshold → absorb into noise.** Quantitative criterion: an effect matters iff it moves the log-likelihood ratio between competing hypotheses by O(1) at the operating SNR (∂LLR/∂θ analysis). At ~5 px character height under strong JPEG compression, effects below ~0.1 px displacement fall beneath the quantization noise floor.

Expected leverage hierarchy: geometry ≫ PSF ≫ resampling/demosaic ≫ codec quantization ≫ noise statistics ≫ low-order illumination ≫ everything else.

### 4.5 Multi-frame fusion (video)

Conditioned on s, frames are independent draws from the forward model with per-frame nuisances. Exact fusion is log-likelihood summation:

L(s) = Σᵢ log ∫ p(yᵢ | s, θᵢ) p(θᵢ) dθᵢ

Consequences:

- Voting, confidence-weighted voting, and logit averaging (used by all top ICPR teams) are lossy approximations of this sum; per-frame hard decisions discard most of the evidence in low-SNR frames, whereas the sum accumulates weak evidence.
- Error probability decays exponentially in frame count (Chernoff): LLR between true plate and nearest competitor grows linearly in N.
- Degradation diversity across frames (sub-pixel phase, varying blur direction, varying pose) is information, not noise — different projections of the plate's spectrum, including above-Nyquist content (the mechanism underlying dither/drizzle in astronomical imaging). Unlike classical multi-frame super-resolution, no shared reconstruction is ever built: frames meet only at the hypothesis level, so a badly registered frame contributes a flat likelihood term instead of corrupting a fused image. Registration failure degrades gracefully.
- **Self-audit for free:** under a correct forward model, per-frame posteriors must scatter around the truth consistently with their per-frame widths (chi-squared testable). Excess disagreement = detected model mismatch on the very observation being decoded, flagged before a confident wrong answer can be emitted.

### 4.6 Calibration

Confidence is a marginal likelihood ratio, not a softmax temperature artifact. A flat trellis posterior is a *proof* that the observation contains insufficient information, distinguishable from a peaked-but-wrong posterior caused by model mismatch (via §4.5). This directly targets the Confidence Gap metric the ICPR organizers identified as underserved.

### 4.7 The ideal-observer bound (primary scientific contribution)

The bound is defined by the **complete forward likelihood** (exact enumeration per §4.2(a)), not by any factorized decoder. Running the exact ideal observer on synthetically degraded data with known ground truth yields the **fundamental accuracy-vs-degradation curve**: maximum achievable recognition rate as a function of character pixel height × blur × codec quality × frame count. The pairwise/augmented-trellis decoders sit at measured, reported distances below this curve (the factorization gap); their curves are model-conditional achievable performance, and only the exact enumeration is claimed as the ceiling. All bound claims are additionally conditional on forward-model correctness; the §5 mitigations and §4.5 consistency test bound that conditionality empirically. Every leaderboard number then becomes a statement of statistical efficiency relative to the bound (the standard ideal-observer-analysis move from visual psychophysics, apparently never applied to LRLPR). This deliverable is robust to the system's own leaderboard performance and requires no restricted dataset access to establish methodologically.

## 5. Known Failure Mode and Mitigation

**Model mismatch makes generative methods confidently wrong.** If the true degradation includes unmodeled structure (JPEG blocking, demosaic zippering, rolling-shutter shear, plate frames, dirt, retroreflective angular response), the likelihood is evaluated under the wrong noise model and the argmax can be stably wrong. This is empirically documented in the redaction-attack literature (Unredacter's authors report sub-pixel rendering-engine differences, character bleed across pixelation grids, and font variation breaking naive render-and-compare). Mitigations, in order:

1. Codec-aware likelihoods: evaluate residuals in DCT-quantization space (cf. compression-informed forensic transformers, Moussa et al.).
2. Marginalization over a forward-model *family* rather than a point model (parametric PSF × codec grid × photometric nuisance).
3. The §4.5 consistency test as a runtime mismatch detector.
4. Hybrid completion: a small learned residual model on top of the physical render (render the physics you know, learn the residual you don't). This is the only stage requiring training data.

Note the field's existing high-fidelity ISP simulation literature ("unprocessing" pipelines for RAW restoration: scene radiance → optics → Bayer sampling → shot/read noise → demosaic → tone map → chroma subsample → DCT quantization) provides validated, open forward-model components. The proposal repurposes this machinery as a likelihood engine rather than a training-data augmenter.

## 6. Prior Art Located So Far

**Direct concept, adjacent domain (text redaction):**
- Hill, Zhou, Saul, Shacham — "On the (In)effectiveness of Mosaicing and Blurring as Tools for Document Redaction" (PETS 2016): HMM decoding of pixelized text. The trellis idea, in simplified degradation.
- Depix (Sipke Mellema), DepixHMM (open implementation of Hill et al.), Unredacter (Dan Petro / Bishop Fox, 2022): render-and-compare recovery of pixelated screenshots.

**Plates — components present, assembled in the opposite direction:**
- Classical template-matching ALPR (surveyed in Anagnostopoulos et al.; various): correlation of raw templates against (near-clean) images. Abandoned due to fragility under lighting/degradation — degradation was never moved into the template side.
- Rendering-based synthetic plate generation for *training data* (e.g., arXiv:2209.14448, rendering pipeline for improved LPR; Xerox patent US8588470 on synthetic plate rendering for similarity learning). Simulator pointed forward, never used as a likelihood engine.
- Forensic probabilistic LPR: Schirrmacher et al., "Benchmarking Probabilistic Deep Learning Methods for License Plate Recognition" (T-ITS 2023); Moussa et al., "Forensic License Plate Recognition with Compression-Informed Transformers" (ICIP 2022). Closest community and closest calibration goals; still discriminative learned models.
- MF-LPR² (Na et al., CVIU 2025): multi-frame restoration via optical flow, explicitly rejecting generative pretrained priors due to artifact risk (supports the anti-hallucination framing; still reconstruction-then-recognition).
- LP-LLM (arXiv:2601.09116, 2026): VLM-based degraded plate recognition; notes VLMs lack explicit structural priors (character position/count/order) — priors the trellis enforces exactly.
- ICPR 2026 LRLPR competition report (arXiv:2604.22506): full survey of current SOTA approaches; all discriminative.

**Confirmed by deep-research review (July 2026) — now central prior art, not footnotes:**
- **Kopec & Chou, "Document Image Decoding Using Markov Source Models" (PAMI 1994)** and lineage: the ancestral idea — message source, renderer, noisy channel, MAP decoding via Viterbi-like algorithm for document images. The present work is a descendant, distinguished by the modern optics–sensor–ISP–codec channel, discrete/continuous joint inference, and hypothesis-level multi-frame fusion.
- **Bulan et al.:** joint plate segmentation and recognition with an HMM and Viterbi decoding. Kills any broad "Viterbi for plates" novelty claim; the surviving specific claim is blur-induced local likelihood factorization over *physically rendered and degraded* hypotheses, with measured factorization gap against exact enumeration.
- **Hsieh et al.:** direct single- and multi-character template matching against plates ~7 px high without segmentation, using resized and photometrically normalized templates. Dangerously close to N1; distinguished (pending verification) by the absence of an optics/sensor/ISP/codec likelihood — normalization-and-correlate rather than render-through-channel.
- **EM-based joint symbol and blur estimation for barcodes (2011):** the underlying discrete-hypothesis + continuous-nuisance joint-inference move exists in a structurally adjacent domain. N3 survives only as the plate-specific instantiation; the barcode work is central prior art.

**Honest lineage statement for the paper:** Document Image Decoding + blurred-plate template search + blind barcode decoding + ideal-observer psychophysics + modern camera simulation, assembled for severe multi-frame ALPR. The claim is the assembly, the channel fidelity, the fusion rule, and the bound — not the inference principle.

**Observed citation gap:** no located ALPR paper cites Hill et al., Depix, or Unredacter. The redaction-attack and forensic-ALPR communities appear disconnected.

## 7. Novelty Claims Requiring Verification (primary reviewer task)

Claims below are annotated with verdicts from the first deep-research pass (July 2026); wording has been narrowed accordingly. Second-pass reviewers should attack the *narrowed* forms.

**N1 (narrowed — probably survives).** No published work decodes license plates by rendering candidates through a physically faithful camera forward model (optics + sensor + ISP + codec) and scoring under the resulting likelihood. Nearest prior art: Hsieh et al. (template matching at ~7 px with resized, photometrically normalized templates — correlation against normalized templates, not render-through-channel likelihood). Verification task: confirm Hsieh et al. and any follow-ups do not model the acquisition channel.

**N2 (broad claim killed — narrowed form only).** Viterbi/HMM plate recognition exists (Bulan et al.); generative image decoding with channel models exists (Kopec & Chou DID). Surviving claim: blur-induced pairwise/k-th-order likelihood factorization over physically rendered-and-degraded hypotheses, with the factorization gap measured against exact enumeration (§4.2). Verification task: any DID-lineage work with a camera-pipeline (vs. scanner/bilevel) channel model.

**N3 (underlying move killed — plate-specific form may survive).** EM-based joint symbol/blur estimation exists for barcodes (2011). Surviving claim: the plate instantiation with full nuisance vector (PSF + photometrics + homography + rolling shutter + codec state) and the scene-structure self-calibration channel (§4.3). Verification task: any extension of the barcode approach to text/plates, and how commercial blurred-barcode decoders work internally.

**N4 (likely survives).** No located ALPR work fuses video frames by per-hypothesis log-likelihood summation with per-frame nuisance marginalization; the field does restoration-then-recognition, learned aggregation, or voting/logit averaging. Verification task: multi-frame likelihood-level fusion in any known-alphabet recognition domain (MRZ, container codes, serials).

**N5 (strongest surviving claim — primary paper).** No located ideal-observer / Bayes-optimal accuracy-vs-degradation bound for ALPR, and no efficiency analysis of existing recognizers against such a bound, despite the methodology being standard in letter-identification psychophysics and medical imaging. Verification task: exhaustive check of Geisler/Pelli/Tjan lineage and medical-imaging ideal-observer literature for any ALPR or scene-text application.

**N6 (probably survives in-domain).** Uncertainty estimation for flagging erroneous ALPR predictions exists; a cross-frame posterior-consistency test derived from the generative acquisition model does not appear to. Verification task: posterior predictive checks used as runtime model-mismatch detectors in any recognition pipeline.

Suggested search directions where refutation is most likely: (a) ideal-observer analysis of character/letter recognition in visual psychophysics (Geisler; Pelli; Tjan et al. — "efficiency of letter identification" literature) — the *bound methodology* certainly exists for human-vision comparisons; the question is whether anyone applied it to ALPR benchmarks; (b) German forensic image analysis groups (Riess, Erlangen; Maier; Moussa; Seiler) — TIFS, Forensic Science International: Digital Investigation, AVSS; (c) pre-deep-learning Bayesian OCR and document-degradation modeling (Kanungo's document degradation models; Sarkar; Kopec & Chou's Document Image Decoding — **Kopec & Chou DID is likely the closest prior art in spirit: communication-theoretic decoding of document images with explicit channel models and Viterbi decoding, 1994, PAMI**; verify whether anyone extended DID to natural-scene/camera-pipeline degradations or plates); (d) astronomy/remote-sensing "matched filter detection of known targets below the resolution limit"; (e) patent literature beyond US8588470.

**A finding that any single claim N1–N6 is already published does not kill the project** — the assembled system + benchmark + bound would still be new — but it changes the paper's framing and citations, and N5 in particular is the claim the primary paper rests on.

## 10. Ethics Note

This work makes plate recovery possible from footage currently considered unusable, which has surveillance implications beyond the forensic/law-enforcement framing. Mitigating considerations: the capability asymmetry favors well-resourced actors already (commercial ALPR, forensic tooling); publishing the *bound* primarily informs the community about what is and is not recoverable (including negative results — proving certain degradations are information-theoretically safe, the same public value the redaction-attack papers provided); and calibrated confidence with honest "insufficient information" verdicts is a direct countermeasure to the current forensic failure mode of overconfident enhancement. The paper should engage with this explicitly, in the tradition of the redaction literature's disclosure framing. Privacy note from the ICPR organizers: Brazilian plates are not publicly linked to owner identity.
